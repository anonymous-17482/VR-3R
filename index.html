Abstract

       
                   

We present VR-3R, a zero-shot 3D scene reconstruction framework that enables immersive VR interaction from a single real-world image. The increasing demand for mixed-reality applications, including memory recollection and remote space exploration, highlights the importance of reconstructing 3D environments from arbitrary photographs. Despite advances in single-image 3D scene generation, existing methods remain limited by incomplete background recovery under occlusion and inaccurate object placement caused by monocular depth estimation. VR-3R addresses these challenges by integrating large-scale model priors to reconstruct high-quality 3D scenes without additional training. The framework incorporates VLM-based foreground–background separation and depth alignment with unprojected point clouds, achieving both scene-level consistency and detailed object recovery. Experiments on the 3D- FRONT dataset and in-the-wild real images demonstrate that VR- 3R generates realistic backgrounds and accurate 3D models across diverse scenarios. The method improves Chamfer Distance by over 23% and F-score by more than 6% at the object level, and achieves 12% and 18% gains in FID and FID-CLIP at the scene level. Our framework reconstructs interactive and immersive VR spaces from a single image, enabling practical deployment in real-world applications.
